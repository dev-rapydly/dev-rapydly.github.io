Rapydly believes software should be clean, beautiful, and only written once. We've engineered a suite of software as a service products to manage your data, and solve some of the most common problems encountered in medium to large enterprises.

Tell me more

Simplify.

We believe your digital architecture (much like software) should be clean and simple. We make it easy to concatenate various data sources (feeds, CSV/TXT files, legacy databases, etc) into modern modular components, and expose that data by "easy-to-integrate", and standardises endpoints. Whether you need a one-off data dump, or daily processes to refresh data, we can concatonate your data and expose it via a single, easy to use endpoint.

Maintain.

We believe your data should be easy to maintain, and fully configurable. Our software allows you to fully control what the data looks like by offering a beautiful Back Office user interface. Whether it's updating the name of the data source, or updating the attribute names on that data, our Back Office allows you to have full control of your data.

Visualise.

We believe your data is valuable, and the best way to appreciate it to visualise it in beautiful ways. Our software helps you and others see your data in any (and every way) possible. From simple tables, to displaying complex spacial data on a map, we can offer almost any visualisation in a single, beautiful, easy to use user interface.

FAQs.

How are these products hosted?
Because a lot of legacy systems will always remain On-Premise, we believe you should be able to chose where you use our software. Rapydly's deployment model allows you to host the applications inside your company's digital architecture, so you decide if you want is deployed to your Cloud or On-Prem.
What stack is the software?
The DataManager API is a .NET Core 2.1 project, and the DataManager UI and Datahub are 100% front end Node.js apps.
How does the pricing model work?
We currently offer Rapydly products with no setup costs, so you only start paying once it's been setup and deployed to your architecture. Invoices are issued at month end, and pricing is based on the number of nodes (tables) you have in your managed database. We do not cap the number of read/writes, or the number of rows in tables. We suggest clients have multiple instances of Rapydly products to account for DEV/UAT/PROD environments, however we do price on a "per-instance" basis.
